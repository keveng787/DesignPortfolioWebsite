For this design project, I was tasked to design a social feature for the Spotify music app. I explored how music streaming could become more social by incorporating user-generated playlists and communities. The goal was to help users discover new music through shared interests, offering an alternative to algorithm-driven recommendations. I began by auditing both Spotify and other social media apps like Instagram and SoundCloud to understand how content sharing and community features were implemented across platforms. This informed the design choices for social features within Spotify. After user interviews, I conducted a usability audit to identify areas for improvement in Spotifyâ€™s social functionality. I then moved to brainstorming and sketching ideas for a new community-based feature. Through iterative design, starting with low-fidelity sketches and paper prototypes, I tested and refined the experience, focusing on accessibility and ease of use. Continuous rounds of user testing helped improve the final high-fidelity prototype in Figma, following the design patterns of Spotify's current interface but enhanced with new social features like playlist sharing and user-generated communities.
